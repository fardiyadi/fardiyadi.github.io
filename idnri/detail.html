Supply Chain Disruption

The improvement of many powerful digital platforms will replace the traditional manufacturing supply chains. Nowadays, new platform business models are disrupting the established supply chain structures which is now beingcovered by new digital platforms. Many new digital platforms are now connecting suppliers, buyers, technology developers, and service providers. One of the examples of supply chain disruption in Indonesia is how a super app like GO-JEK is providing not only for ride-hailing service, but also for logistic service. The existence of a startup which provides one stop service is a thread for conventional supply chains service which has been existing.

Accelerating Sustainability

The advancement of technology has to be balanced with maintaining the sustainability of our environment. Based on the World Economic Forum, about half of the world’s total energy consumption and about 20% of global greenhouse gas emissions are attributed to the manufacturing sector, means that a sustainable production system has to become the global standard immediately. According to communications and sustainability consultant, Nick Elliott, air pollution in Jakarta has reached an alarming level and has become an “invisible threat”. In addition to Jakarta’s notoriously bad traffic, Greenpeace believes the city’s industries is one of the main reasons towards the fact that in 2018, Jakarta ranked as the most polluted city in south-east Asia . Advance manufacturing and production is a solution to some industries’ problems but we also need to see the downside and the effects which its caused.

Evolving Manufacturing Skills

As the technology of manufacturing is improving, the technical skills which followed it needs to be improved as well although there will be many job roles which have to be replaced by artificial intelligence and machine learning. We all need to be ready to develop the skills which require the advancement of manufacturing and production. Based on International Labour Organization (ILO), to enhance skills development and improve youth employment in Indonesia, it is key to engage the industry in the design and delivery of vocational education and training. It is hoped that Indonesia continues to strengthen its effort to improve the quality of work-based learning, particularly in apprenticeships as a viable response to skills mismatch.
