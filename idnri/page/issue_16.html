Infrastructure in a Maritime Country

Global Issue

Back to previous page

Summary

Indonesia’s government is now paying more attention to the infrastructure development of Indonesia as its plans are including more than $400 billion in building projects, constructing 25 airports and new power plants as the government seeks the economic growth. The infrastructure development is more specifically directed for connecting the rural areas and between islands in Indonesia. This national program of building infrastructure is a key plank to boost economic growth and spread wealth beyond the main island-powerhouse of Java, where the capital is located.

Infrastructure Technology and Innovation

Infrastructure in Indonesia can't be separated by the development of technology. Although the development of building infrastructure is becoming the main program of the government, right now, Indonesia still has less understanding in construction technology which used to build infrastructure projects such as buildings and bridges. This issue will not only be a problem in the time frame of finishing the infrastructure projects, but also potentially create future problem in the sustainability of the physical buildings or bridges.

Private Capital in Infrastructure

Indonesia has sought to increase private sector participation in major infrastructure projects, unveiling a series of new developments and announcing a shift in funding policy that could open the door to more international and domestic investors. Some infrastructure projects in Indonesia will be undertaken in partnership with state-owned enterprises (SOEs), cover a number of sectors, including transport, energy and industry. While Indonesia’s expansive infrastructure programme and the push to increase private sector participation is providing significant investment opportunities, industry figures say the country needs to improve the execution of large-scale developments if it is to attract investors outside of Asia.

Corruption and Infrastructure

Infrastructure programs are prone to be corrupted as it involved many stakeholders and private sectors. The competition among providers of construction services often leads corruption. Based on a research strudy, the characteristics of construction projects involved in corruption is dominated by projects which, are owned by government that hire private company as the contractor. While the vulnerability characteristics of construction projects involved in corruption are: occurs at construction phase due to contractual violation; sequential acts of corruption is begin with the difference between the volume of work that resulted in payments, which is not in accordance with progress reports; and the parties involved is Committing Officer (Pejabat Pembuat Komitmen/PPK).
