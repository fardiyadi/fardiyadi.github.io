Retail, Consumer Goods, and Lifestyle

Back to previous page

Summary

As the overall economy grew between 6,2% and 6,5% per year since 2010, the rising personal incomes are lifting millions of Indonesians into the middle class every year, and FMCGs, which include groceries, pharmaceuticals, toiletries and household care products, are among the first items they spend more money on. The spread of retail outlets throughout the country, like modern supermarkets, convenience stores, and pharmacies, increases options for FMCG sales into regions so far served mainly by traditional markets. Increased connectivity between Islands and the available service for online shopping are also helping regional centres narrow the development gap with the capital. Urbanization also tends to promote new lifestyles, creating demand for products consumers may previously have considered unnecessary.

Sustainable Consumption and Green Living

In 2018, Indonesia's government implemented the systemic change related to Sustainable Consumption and Production in government, private sectors, and the society. That were proven by the implementation of some government's programs such as Eco-Office, Sistem Verifikasi Legalitas Kayu (SVLK), and the Trash Bank initaitive. Those programs were implemented to support the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The concept of green living is now also being a trend as many influencers and celebrities were endorsing some products which support the concept of eco-living.

Distribution Channel and Point of Sales

As Indonesia is the largest archipelagic country in the world, where two-third of its territory is water, this country depends mainly on maritime transportation to boost its domestic and international trade. In 2019, The Ministry of Transportation has recorded a 17% increase on national logistics distribution volume within the last 3 years; predominantly due to the increase of not only port-to-port distribution, but also end-to-end distribution, which directly reaches out to consumers. With the improvement of infrastructure and mobility in Indonesia, the future of the logistics industry in Indonesia is promising.
