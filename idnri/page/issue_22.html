Transportation and Logistics

Summary

The logistics industry plays a vital role in supporting and enhancing the distribution flow of most manufactured products to all areas across Indonesia. Based on the prediction from Michael Page Indonesia, the future of the logistics industry in Indonesia is promising. With the combined demand of major agriculture commodities exports valued at approximately IDR 500 trillion and also eCommerce transactions valued at approximately IDR 77 trillion so far, the demand on the logistics industry as the vehicle to mobilize business-to-business, business-to-consumer, and consumer-to-consumer delivery is also projected to increase regardless of the tariff situations mentioned above.

Energy Efficiency and Mobility

Indonesia was able to offset 23% of the impact of growing activity thanks to energy efficiency improvements between 2000 and 2017. By adopting the cost-effective policies of the Efficient World Strategy, Indonesia could reduce CO2 emissions by 120 Mt CO2-eq by 2040 compared to expected trends and save households USD 7 billion due to reduced energy bills, as well as providing 26 million more families with access to clean cooking. There is still a lack of development in advancing energy efficiency in mobility in Indonesia.

Supply Chain and Infrastructure

Supply Chain Indonesia analyzed the growth of the logistics sector-driven was supported by the growth of the non-oil and gas processing Industry especially the food and trade industry due to the increase in the production of domestic and imported goods. In addition to the agricultural sector which experienced growth, the construction sector also grew with increased infrastructure development. To improve the growth of the supply chain industry, a collaboration between logistics service providers needs to be improved, also between logistics service providers and logistics facility/infrastructure providers, such as ports and airports.
