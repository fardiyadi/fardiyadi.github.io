Digital Economy and Society

Global Issue

Back to previous page

Summary

In the end of 2018, half of the world’s population was connected to the internet. Based on Indonesia Millennial Report 2019, 79% of Indonesian millennials checked their phones at least 1 minute after they woke up in the morning each day. The digital era and the extreme growth of technology are disrupting almost all our lives’ aspects, especially in the development of our economy within the society. While digital technology has the potential to enhance economies, societies, and the environment, it also has potential risks emerge as it influences the concentration of power, wealth, and social instability.
Image : mural24.co.uk

Adoption and Access

Based on the latest survey of the Association of Internet Service Providers in Indonesia (APJII), the number of Internet users in Indonesia reached 171.17 million individuals at the start of 2019, or approximately 64.8 percent of the total Indonesian population. The dramatic levels of technology disruption in Indonesia will influence many sectors. Based on World Economic Forum, establishing new norms of ethical behavior regarding digital technology, and attaining higher levels of customer trust, will be critical for a successful digital transformation.

Behavior Disruption and Transformation

The rapid growth of technology development is influencing the way society's behavior of transactions. Based on Indonesia Millennial Report 2019, 36.7% millennials were using e-wallet, e-money, mobile and internet banking. The development of technology is definitely being one of the main reasons towards the society's change behavior towards doing transactiion within their daily basis.
