Indonesia is facing a challenge to maintaining a stable democracy as many issues related to it happened in 2019. Institutional Pluralism

Pluralism in Indonesia is not only about its diverse religions among the citizens. Indonesia is seeing as a country which has strong institutions, a relatively free media in all, a constitutional commitment to pluralism, and a longstanding liberal tradition that allows strong social movements to thrive. However, Indonesia is still facing some challenges and unfinished cases related to pluralism found accross the country.

Regional Autonomy and Decentralization

Since the start of the reform in the late 1990s, Indonesia has implemented an ambitious decentralization policy. The decentralized government system has been implemented for almost two decades. Based on a research, the decentralization practice in Indonesia began to show positive outcomes, which was signified by a declining trend of regional disparity and spatial fragmentation. It is argued that institutional rearrangement has become a crucial aspect in resolving the regional issues and, thus, improving regional development.

Democracy & Government

Emerging from the ashes of authoritarianism, Indonesia has built a trusted and resilient electoral system over the past 20 years. The election of Jokowi who is a political outsider as president in 2014 was a high point for proving the robust and contestable electoral system to be the sign of a real democracy. Nowadays, some of the biggest challenges to democracy in Indonesia today are a result of the growth of identity politics and the associated rise of a more pious and strident attitude toward religion.
