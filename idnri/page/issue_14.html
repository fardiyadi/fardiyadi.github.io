Harmony in a Multicultural Society

Back to previous page

Summary

As Unity in Diversity or Bhinneka Tunggal Ika acts like Indonesia's national motto, it is prominent to highlight the multiculturalism within the nation. Indonesia has more than 17,000 islands - contains a population numbering around 255 million people with extremely diverse in cultural, ethnic, religious, and linguistic aspects which also influence each lifestyle of the citizen. It is crucial to maintain the stability and peace among the diverse nation of Indonesia in order to create harmony in daily basis.

Social Stability and Progress

Indonesia has been transformed from a country with a tiny social elite and a mass of impoverished peasants to a rapidly urbanising society with new social groups less willing to trade political rights for personal prosperity. The crisis has raised the possibility that many ordinary Indonesian people may join in spontaneous or organised movements of mass protest, perhaps even a 'people's power' movement. It is extremely crucial to maintain the unity in Indonesia as there are many factors that can be a threat to social stability, such as political issue, economic growth, and the disruption which created by technology.

Nation-Building and Tolerance

The development of Indonesia is highly influenced by the way its citizen interact with each other. To overcome the differences which possibly emerge within the society, we need tolerance to create a cohesive society. Fortunately, the concept of tolerance is not a new thing for Indonesia's young generation. In our survey, we broke down some behaviors which manifested from 5 types of tolerance. From 5 types of tolerance, which are social tolerance, thought tolerance, religious tolerance, racial tolerance, and sexual tolerance, Indonesian millennials were mostly choosing thought tolerance behaviors in defining their own definition of tolerance.
