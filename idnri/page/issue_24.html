Workforce and Employment

Back to previous page

Summary

As technology and digital era is rapidly growing, the world of work is changing fast as well. It’s important to recognize and map the new work models or adjusting the required education and skills which our labours will need. With the Fourth Industrial Revolution, there is a greater variety of adult training and learning opportunities which is necessary to strengthen the quality of our human resources. Not only the skills, the characteristics which owned by millennials will be a challenge to successfully hiring global talents. Based on Indonesia Millennial Report 2019, the ideal length of working period in a company for millennials was only 2–3 years. While what make millennials would stay in a company were the existence of self-development opportunities, a collaborative teammates, the freedom to be creative, and flexible working hours.

Disruption to Job and Skills

Based on the World Economic Forum, in the UK, artificial intelligence will eliminate approximately 7 million jobs by 2037 although based on a report by PWC, there will be an estimated amount of 7.2 million new jobs in a country during the same year, especially in the sector of healthcare, science, and education. In Indonesia, based on Statistics Indonesia Survey (BPS), in February 2019, the amount of employed citizens were increasing 2.24 million people compared with the same month last year. The sector which mostly had the increased numbers of employees was in accomodation, food, and beverage. The development of employment in Indonesia will disrupted by the emergence of startups alongside the rapid growth of technology.

Labour Market Demographics

As Indonesia has the bonus demographic, the society which is partly fulfilled by the youths require different approaches when it comes to develop talent pools. Based on International Labor Organization (ILO) survey in 2016, soft skills are among the most important skills that the employers consider the potential employees should have. The employers revealed that they look for workers that are skilled with three important skills which are technical knowledge, teamwork, and communication. Gender equality is still being an issue towards Indonesia’s employment as based on Statistics Indonesia Survey (BPS), there was an increase in the labor force participation rate among women to 55.4% in 2018 from 55% the previous year, although the challenges for female workers in the workplace remain high.

Link and Match Phenomenon

While developing talent pools towards business disruption, Indonesia is also still developing the ‘link and match’ program which is a exploration of talent’s competencies which are needed by the job market. In 2019, The Industry Ministry said that it had linked 143 companies in Jakarta and Banten with 292 vocational schools (SMKs) in order to educate the students to become skilled workers needed by the relevant industries. Until present, approximately more than 1500 vocational schools have been linked to more than 500 industries in East Java, Central Java, West Java, Aceh, North and West Sumatra, Riau, Riau Islands, Jakarta, and Banten. This “link and match” program is implemented according to the Presidential Instruction No. 9/2016 on SMK revitalization.
