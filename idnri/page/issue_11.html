Future of Health and Healthcare

Global Issue

Back to previous page

Summary

Indonesia is home to 260 million people and is one of the fastest-growing pharmaceutical markets in Asia. Indonesia’s pharmaceutical market is ranked as the largest market in the ASEAN regions. According to Frost and Sullivan, Indonesia’s healthcare expenditure is projected to reach USD 60.6 billion in 2018 with a growth rate of 14.9%. Universal health coverage was introduced in 2014 with the Jaminan Kesehatan Nasional (JKN), which now includes 76% of the population, the largest single-payer scheme in the world. Indonesia needs to always increase the quality of healthcare for its citizen, aligns with the development of infrastructure and technology.
Image : 99insight.com

Healthcare Technology

Improving the competitiveness of the pharmaceutical industry and medical devices, as well as its resilience and national independence is a mandatory strategy to support the policy and national strategy. The healthcare equipment and medical device market in Indonesia remain attractive for foreign medical technology suppliers. The Indonesian medical devices market has shown strong growth over the last decade, reaching a volume of about US$ 1 billion. It is widely expected to continue to grow at a rate of 12% annually based on steady per capita increases and the initiative of Indonesian governments to extend health care coverage to everybody.

Environmental Health and Climate Change

In the 2019 edition of the Climate Change Performance Index, Indonesia is ranked 38th and is thus classified as a low-performing country. Indonesia is rated low on Renewable Energy. National experts criticize the fact that there is yet no effective support mechanism for renewables, which is also reflected in a comparably low increase in the share of renewable energy in recent years. This is one of the main reasons why national experts give a very low rating to Indonesia’s national climate policy. The climate condition in Indonesia is extremely influencing the quality of healthcare in Indonesia and how it can tackle down the healthcare issue in Indonesia.
