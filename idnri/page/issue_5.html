Business and Entrepreneurship

Back to previous page

Summary

The development of entrepreneurship in Indonesia can be influenced by leveraging the power of the internet and other digital technologies. Google and Temasek projected that Indonesia’s startup landscape is one of the most dynamic in Asia as they projected that Indonesia’s digital economy market value will triple from US$27 billion in 2018 to US$100 billion by 2025. Whether entrepreneurship manifests in the form of a new startup or as a regenerating force within an established company, it is one of the most fundamental aspects to drive Indonesia’s economic value creation. Based on Indonesia Millennial Report 2019, 69.1% millennials were interested to start a business which means 7 out of 10 millennials had the entrepreneurial drive. Not only for Indonesian millennials, based on a survey showed that among all Southeast Asia countries, Indonesians had the most interest in entrepreneurship (96%).

Human Capital Mindsets

The emergence and the growth of entrepreneurship in Indonesia will be influenced by the quality of its human capital. We need to develop entrepreneurial skills among millennials, such as the skill to effectively pitching ideas, providing inspirational leadership that can attract talent, and having the operational management skill in order to develop the Indonesia’s entrepreneurial landscape. Based on a research, the most challenging part for Indonesian millennials in starting a new business was their lack of creativity and professional skills, especially in entrepreneurship. Entrepreneurial skills among millennials need to be embedded through their education system.

Corporate Intrapreneruship

The development of entrepreneurship, especially startups, have been disrupted many large corporations in Indonesia. Nowadays, many large businesses are adopting an entrepreneurial mindset as they begin to embrace innovation on their products and services. Corporate-sponsored hackathons, corporate accelerator programs, and innovation labs are some of the programs which now are being developed by many large companies in order to grow the “intrapreneurship” mindset among its business models.

Family Businesses

Not only for the global economy, the ability of the family business sector to thrive is vital for the long-term growth of Indonesia’s economy. Based on a survey conducted by PWC in 2018, 22% of family businesses in Indonesia were in manufacturing sector and 44% of Indonesian family businesses have one dominant owner. The growth among Indonesian family business in 2018 has increased compared to previous years. Nevertheless, the key challenges for Indonesian family businesses in the next years ahead are access to the right skills and capabilities, the need to innovate in order to keep ahead, the economic environment and competition. Furthermore, innovation and profitability are also key.

Nano, Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises

Based on the survey report by Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation in 2018, MSMEs in Indonesia provided 89% of private-sector employment and contributed 60% of the GDP. Indonesian millennials are also taking an important role towards the development of MSMEs in Indonesia as 40% of MSMEs founders in Indonesia aged 34 years old and under. Developing a strong coordination between the private sectors and the government, supporting in-school entrepreneurship training, and investing in information and communications technology (ICT) infrastructure are some of the ways to support MSMEs in Indonesia.
