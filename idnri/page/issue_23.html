Travel and Tourism

Back to previous page

Summary

At present, Indonesia's tourism sector contributes to approximately 4% of the total economy. In 2019, the government of Indonesia wants to double the numbers to 8% of GDP, an ambitious target which implies that in the next 4 years, the number of visitors needs to double to around 20 million. In order to achieve this target, the government will focus on improving Indonesia's infrastructure (including information and communication technology infrastructure), access, health & hygiene and also increasing online promotion campaigns or marketing abroad. The government also revised the free-visa access policy in 2015 to attract more foreign tourists.

Experiential Travel

While this can make for a stress-free and relaxing escape, it doesn’t offer the excitement and enrichment of visiting off-the-beaten-path locations. Here, picture hopping around Indonesia visiting remote beaches and getting to know the local cultures along the way. Experiential travel rejects the convenient yet watered-down travel experience, and instead gets back to the roots of travel. Traveling used to be synonymous with adventure, and was full of the thrill that comes along with traversing untrodden territory and a subsequent sense of uncertainty. Not knowing beforehand what you’re going to encounter around every corner allows travelers’ childlike senses of wonder to be reignited.

Tourism Infrastructure and Local Development

Based on Travel & Tourism Competitiveness Report from World Economic Forum, although Indonesia has a lot of tourism sites, Indonesia does not put enough emphasis on environmental sustainability (resulting in deforestation and endangering endangered species, while only a small amount is treated). The report also mentions worries about safety and security, especially business losses due to terrorism. The lack of proper infrastructure in Indonesia is an ongoing problem, not only because it greatly increases logistics costs, making the investment climate less attractive, but also reduces the travel journey for tourism. Infrastructure outside Bali and Jakarta is is inadequate, especially in Eastern Indonesia due to lack of airports, ports, roads and hotels. Lack of connectivity within and between islands means that there are a large number of regions in Indonesia with tourism potential that cannot be visited easily.
